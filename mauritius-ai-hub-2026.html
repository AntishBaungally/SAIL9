Connectivity Hub

Three components, one strategic vision

The budget assembles a connectivity hub from three interlocking announcements that together position Mauritius as a trusted base for AI and cloud services serving the Indian Ocean, SADC region, and the India–Africa corridor.

🌐

Subsea Backbone

Participation in Google's America-India Connect programme, secured via PM Ramgoolam's request to PM Modi at the AI Impact Summit in New Delhi. Adds hyperscaler-grade bandwidth resilience strengthening routes towards South Africa, India, and Singapore.

In progress G2G secured
☁️

AI & Cloud Hub

Government actively engaging leading US and European AI and cloud companies to explore AI infrastructure and AI-enabled services serving Mauritius, the Indian Ocean, and SADC. First explicit budget commitment to hosting hyperscaler AI workloads.

Negotiations ongoing
🏙️

Digital Finance Centre

41 arpents adjoining the Cruise Terminal, Port Louis. Major urban regeneration including residential, tourism, commerce, entertainment, leisure, and a dedicated Digital Finance Centre. Combined with the Harbour Bridge project.

Planning stage
Private Sector Layer

National telecom operator — 3-year investment programme

Adding two new submarine cables — T4 and SEACOM 2.0 — to significantly increase current capacity before SAFE cable retires (~2027). Targets: sovereign cloud services, AI and 5.5G, digital payments platform expansion across Africa–Asia corridor. "Build in Mauritius, Scale Across the Continent" approach.

Key programmes

National AI Learning Platform

Rs 25 million allocated for a national cloud-hosted platform offering structured training and certification. Also hosts the AI Innovation Start-Up Programme supporting youth entrepreneurship. This is both a government investment and a procurement opportunity for AI platform providers.

Rs 25M · Procurement opp.

Champion of AI Programme

Developed in collaboration with the Government of India. Builds a network of AI leaders driving responsible AI adoption, training, and organisational transformation. Part of the deepening India-Mauritius bilateral relationship.

India collaboration

MyT-GPT education scale-up

Built on a successful pilot in 10 schools. Government will scale AI-enabled learning across the education system "in a safe and responsible manner." Represents a pilot-to-scale methodology that was absent in the 2025–2026 framing.

Evidence-based scale-up

National AI Guideline

Sector-specific guideline issued for governance and responsible use of AI in higher education and the civil service. More operationalised than the broad FAIR Guidelines — directly tied to the budget's deployment programmes.

HE + Civil service
Why this matters for investors

The 50,000-person programme creates a demand-side pull that de-risks AI platform investment: the government is simultaneously the trainer (creating talent supply), the first customer (procuring the platform), and the policy enabler (National AI Learning Platform + Guideline + Champion programme). For a cloud or AI company evaluating Mauritius, the government is signalling as an anchor customer at national scale.

Governance Sandbox

FAIR framework + cyber assurance layer

The governance architecture has matured from the 2025–2026 budget. It now has an ethical framework (FAIR Guidelines), a sector-specific implementation instrument (National AI Guideline), a cybersecurity assurance layer (Rs 13M package), and financial sector-specific governance (BoM Threat Intelligence Platform, Open Banking, stablecoin rules).

🏛️

FAIR Guidelines

Launched 9 April 2026 with UNDP technical support. Vendor-neutral, border-agnostic — imported AI tools face the same scrutiny as domestic systems. Four principles: Fairness, Accountability, Inclusiveness & Integrity, Responsibility.

UNDP-backedNon-binding → legislation
🔐

Rs 13M Cyber Assurance

Three-component package: Cyber Forensic Laboratory (investigative capabilities), national cybersecurity survey (preparedness assessment), and ISMS deployment across government. CERT-MU national fraud reporting mechanism.

Rs 13M allocatedISO 27001 aligned
🏦

BoM Threat Intelligence Platform

Bank of Mauritius rolling out a real-time cybersecurity intelligence sharing platform among banks. Directly relevant for AI companies operating in financial services — establishes the threat-sharing infrastructure AI systems need to operate safely.

Real-time intelBoM-mandated
💱

Stablecoins & Tokenisation

Clear rules for issuance and investment in stablecoins and tokenisation of real-world assets. New Bank of Mauritius Bill + Banking Bill + Resolution Regime — modernising 2004-era legislation. Directly enables AI + digital asset convergence plays.

Legal clarityBills before end 2026
🔄

Open Banking Framework

Secure sharing of customer financial data between licensed banks, VASPs, and authorised FinTech providers. For AI companies, this creates access to structured, consented financial data — the foundation for AI-powered credit scoring, fraud detection, and AML analytics.

Data sharing enabledAI analytics opportunity
⚠️

Persistent gap: MAIC

The Mauritius Artificial Intelligence Council (MAIC) — a 10-member unified AI oversight body recommended since 2018 — remains not fully institutionalised. The AI Unit at MITCI, National AI Guideline, FSC, and BoM work in parallel without a single unified AI governance body.

The Case for Mauritius

Eight strategic advantages for AI & cloud investors

01

Hyperscaler-grade connectivity — being built now

Mauritius is securing participation in Google's America-India Connect programme — the same subsea cable infrastructure Google uses for its own cloud regions. Combined with T4 and SEACOM 2.0 cables being added by the national telecom operator, Mauritius will have multi-cable redundancy with high-capacity routes to India, South Africa, Singapore, and the US. For a cloud or AI provider evaluating a regional node, this is the foundational infrastructure requirement.

Google AI-Connect confirmedT4 + SEACOM 2.0 in progress
02

Investment Tax Credit — 45% over 3 years on AI solutions

Manufacturing and technology companies investing in AI solutions are eligible for a 15% annual tax credit over three years (45% total). Any unrelieved credit carries forward for up to 10 years. Extended until 30 June 2029. For a technology provider capitalising AI infrastructure, AI development tools, or AI-powered products within Mauritius, this is a direct and material offset against total investment cost.

45% total · 3 years10-yr carryforwardUntil June 2029
03

80% income exemption on AI advisory services income

Companies holding the FSC's Robotic & AI Enabled Advisory Services licence benefit from 80% partial exemption on income — effectively reducing the blended corporate tax rate to approximately 3% on qualifying AI revenue. Substance requirements apply: physical presence, local management, and employment of local staff. This is the highest-value incentive for AI-as-a-service platforms and AI advisory businesses establishing a Mauritius hub.

~3% effective rateSubstance test applies
04

Premium Investor Certificate — bespoke incentive package at scale

For investments of Rs 500 million (approx. USD 11 million) or more, the EDB's Premium Investor Certificate provides access to a fully negotiated package of incentives, rebates, exemptions, and preferential tax rates. The PIC was extended in 2025 to cover foreign laboratories and research-related investments — directly relevant for hyperscalers co-locating R&D alongside data centre infrastructure. Includes fast-tracked permitting, work permit access for specialist AI engineers, and EDB relationship management.

Rs 500M+ thresholdNow covers R&D labsNegotiated package
05

Côte d'Or SEZ — dedicated AI and digital investment zone

83 arpents of land in a purpose-built Special Economic Zone at Côte d'Or, targeting AI, digital sectors, and advanced manufacturing. Infrastructure works are being completed and the SEZ incentive scheme is being implemented. Standard SEZ benefits in Mauritius include customs duty exemptions on capital equipment and reduced corporate rates for qualifying exporters. The SEZ also hosts the national Start-Up Hub — creating an innovation ecosystem around any anchor tenant investment.

83 arpentsIncentives being finalisedCustoms duty exemptions
06

10-year income tax holiday for startups (from day of operations)

New startups — including AI ventures and technology joint ventures incorporated in Mauritius — benefit from a 10-year income tax holiday applicable from the day operations commence. This is significantly more generous than in previous years (which counted from incorporation). For an AI company establishing a Mauritius subsidiary, structuring operations under the Start-Up Act framework captures the full decade of exemption. The framework also includes a dedicated start-up labour framework drawing on international best practices.

10-year full holidayFrom day of operations
07

Golden Visa — fast-track residency for AI talent and investors

A new Golden Visa scheme targets high-value sectors explicitly including AI, FinTech, global treasury, biotechnology, and renewable energy. For AI companies needing to import senior AI engineers, infrastructure architects, and cloud specialists, the Golden Visa creates a fast-track residence and work pathway. The visa comes with tax incentives (though not full tax exemption — top personal rate is 35% on income exceeding Rs 12M p.a., a tension the government will need to resolve).

AI sector targetedTax incentives (partial)
08

Government as anchor customer — de-risked demand

The government is simultaneously creating captive demand for AI and cloud services: the Rs 25M National AI Learning Platform procurement, AI tools at the EDB and FSC, the 50,000-person training programme, the 8,000-teacher AI platform, and sectoral AI tools in agriculture (FAREI), healthcare (new AI Unit), and tourism. For a cloud or AI provider, this means government is not just offering fiscal incentives — it is signalling as a first and anchor customer at national scale, substantially de-risking the initial commercial commitment.

Risk Assessment

Honest evaluation — gaps and challenges

The strategy is architecturally sound. The principal risks lie in the translation gap between policy announcement and measurable, time-bound outcomes — a pattern that has affected previous AI policy cycles in Mauritius.

👥

Talent base constraint

At 1.26 million population, Mauritius has limited deep AI engineering talent domestically. The 50,000 skills programme addresses adoption-level proficiency, not hyperscaler-grade infrastructure engineering. Dependence on Golden Visa imports for senior talent.

💰

Top marginal tax rate tension

Revised personal income tax provisions raise the effective top rate to 35% on personal income exceeding Rs 12 million per annum — potentially discouraging high-income AI specialists, appearing inconsistent with Golden Visa objectives.

🏛️

MAIC gap — no unified AI council

The Mauritius AI Council (MAIC), recommended since 2018, remains not fully institutionalised. The AI Unit at MITCI, FSC, BoM, and National AI Guideline operate in parallel without a unified AI oversight body — fragmentation risk for governance.

📊

No performance framework

Beyond the 50,000 skills target and export doubling goal, the budget lacks a comprehensive AI performance dashboard with KPIs, ministerial accountability markers, and reporting timelines. Execution risk without measurement infrastructure.

🔄

Execution track record

PwC's assessment: "This Budget gets the direction right. The open question is whether Mauritius will commit the scale and pace to match it." Budget 2025–2026 commitments (AI Unit, sector tools) are still being implemented at the time of Budget 2026–2027.

⚖️

FAIR Guidelines non-binding

The FAIR Guidelines are currently non-binding with no penalties for non-compliance. While this maintains flexibility, it creates regulatory ambiguity for investors who need certainty about future enforcement — particularly in cross-border data flow scenarios.

Overall assessment

The Mauritius Budget 2026–2027 AI agenda is strategically coherent and the incentive stack is genuinely competitive by regional standards. The budget has materially responded to the critique from 2025–2026 — the 50,000 skills target, the named teacher and student cohorts, and the MyT-GPT evidence base all represent a maturing from intent to execution. The remaining structural weakness is the absence of a fully empowered MAIC and a cross-government AI performance dashboard. For investors: the fiscal case is strong; the execution risk is moderate; the governance environment is maturing but not yet fully stabilised.
