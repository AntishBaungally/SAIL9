Connectivity & Infrastructure — Budget Speech §17–19, §41–46

Three fronts on AI and digitisation

The PM stated government is acting on three fronts: (1) subsea connectivity, (2) positioning as trusted AI/cloud base, (3) investing in people. Paragraphs 17–19 of the Budget Speech are the sole primary source for the connectivity announcements.

🌐

Google America-India Connect

PM Ramgoolam requested PM Modi's assistance at the AI Impact Summit in New Delhi. Mauritius will benefit from participation in this major international subsea connectivity programme, adding resilience to existing systems and strengthening routes towards South Africa, India and Singapore.

Source: BS §18, 19 June 2026

G2G initiative BS §18
☁️

Trusted AI & cloud base

Government is engaging with leading American and European AI and cloud companies to explore opportunities for AI infrastructure and AI-enabled services serving Mauritius, the Indian Ocean and the SADC region. No specific companies were named in the Budget Speech.

Source: BS §19, 19 June 2026

Engagements ongoing BS §19
👥

Investing in people

The PM described investing in people as "most importantly" the third front — covering the education scale-up, the 50,000 skills programme, and the National AI Learning Platform. This front has the most detailed, budgeted commitments in the Speech.

Source: BS §20–34, 19 June 2026

Most detailed/budgeted BS §20
Budget Speech §41–43

Côte d'Or Special Economic Zone

🏗️

83 arpents · High-tech SEZ at Côte d'Or

A new high-tech Special Economic Zone is being set up on 83 arpents of land at Côte d'Or, following the PM's participation at the AI Summit in New Delhi. Infrastructure works are being completed and an SEZ incentive scheme is being implemented. Government expects the SEZ to attract significant high-impact investment in AI, digital sectors, and advanced manufacturing over the next five years.

Infrastructure underwayIncentive scheme being implemented

Export target

Government set an ambitious target of increasing exports of goods from USD 1.5 billion today to USD 3 billion within the next five years. The SEZ is the primary vehicle for this target.

Source: BS §43

Cruise Terminal project including Digital Finance Centre

🏙️

Government is considering a new project over 41 arpents of land adjoining the Cruise Terminal. The project will cut across most key sectors — from residential to tourism to commerce, entertainment and leisure, to a Digital Finance Centre, among others. Together with the Harbour Bridge, it will be a major urban regeneration project.

Responsible AI & Governance — Budget Speech §32–34, §60–63

National AI Guideline, cyber assurance, and financial sector governance

The budget's governance commitments span three areas: an AI guideline for the public sector, a cybersecurity investment package, and financial sector regulatory modernisation including stablecoins, Open Banking, and a bank threat intelligence platform.

Responsible AI — BS §32–34

Oversight and cybersecurity

📋

National AI Guideline

Government will issue a National Artificial Intelligence Guideline for governance and responsible use of AI in higher education and in the civil service. The PM stated: "As we promote AI, we must also retain firm oversight and control over its development and use."

Rs 13 million cybersecurity package

Three components: (1) a Cyber Forensic Laboratory to strengthen investigative capabilities; (2) a national cybersecurity survey to assess preparedness and improve resilience; (3) setting up Information Security Management Systems across Government.

Launched 9 April 2026 by MITCI with UNDP technical support. Grounded in Fairness, Accountability, Inclusiveness and Integrity, and Responsibility. Vendor-neutral and border-agnostic. Currently non-binding, with a stated legislative trajectory.

Financial Sector — BS §59–63

Banking modernisation and digital finance regulation

BoM Threat Intelligence Sharing Platform

The Bank of Mauritius will roll out a Threat Intelligence Sharing Platform to facilitate real-time exchange of cybersecurity intelligence among banks.

Source: BS §60(4)

CERT-MU national fraud reporting mechanism

The Computer Emergency Response Team of Mauritius will establish a national fraud reporting and response mechanism to better protect citizens and businesses from cyber fraud and scams.

Source: BS §60(3)

Stablecoins and tokenisation — clear rules

Clear rules for the issuance and investment in stablecoins, and the tokenisation of real-world assets.

Source: BS §61

Open Banking Framework

An Open Banking Framework will be introduced to enable the secure sharing of customer financial data between licensed banks, virtual asset service providers and authorised fintech providers.

Source: BS §61

New Bank of Mauritius Bill, Banking Bill and Resolution Regime

To be introduced before the end of 2026. Will strengthen governance, sharpen prudential regulation and supervision, enhance consumer protection, and bolster financial stability, while fostering innovation. Current banking legislation dates to 2004.

Source: BS §62–63

⚠ Noted gap: MAIC

The Mauritius Artificial Intelligence Council (MAIC), recommended in the 2018 AI Strategy as a 10-member unified oversight body, was not mentioned in the Budget Speech 2026–2027. The AI Unit at MITCI, the National AI Guideline, the FSC, and the Bank of Mauritius each address AI governance within their respective mandates without a single unified AI oversight body. This structural gap pre-dates this budget and persists.

Source: Mauritius AI Strategy (2018); OECD.AI dashboard entry for Mauritius AI Strategy

Google America-India Connect — hyperscaler-grade subsea connectivity

Mauritius is to benefit from participation in Google's America-India Connect initiative, a major international subsea connectivity programme that can add a new layer of resilience to existing submarine cable systems and strengthen strategic routes towards South Africa, India and Singapore. Secured via PM Ramgoolam's request to PM Modi at the AI Impact Summit in New Delhi.

Source: Budget Speech §18, National Assembly, 19 June 2026

02

Investment Tax Credit — 15% per year × 3 years on AI and patent capital expenditure

Enacted via Finance (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2024 (27 July 2024), effective 1 July 2024. A 15% investment tax credit, spread over three years, applies to capital expenditure incurred in developing artificial intelligence and patents. Any unrelieved credit may be carried forward for 10 years. Extended until 30 June 2029 per MCCI Budget Highlights 2026/27.

Source: Finance (Misc. Provisions) Act 2024; UNCTAD Investment Policy Monitor (measure 4961); MCCI Budget Highlights 2026/27

45% total over 3 years10-yr carryforwardExtended to June 2029
03

80% income exemption — Robotic and AI Enabled Advisory Services licence

Companies holding a Robotic and Artificial Intelligence Enabled Advisory Services licence issued by the Financial Services Commission can claim an 80% exemption on income, subject to meeting substance requirements, effective 1 July 2024. Substance requirements include: physical presence in Mauritius, core income generated in or from Mauritius, local management, and employment of local staff.

Source: Finance (Misc. Provisions) Act 2024; UNCTAD Investment Policy Monitor (measure 4961); FSC Mauritius

80% exemptionSubstance test applies
04

Double deduction on AI capital and recurrent expenditure (Finance Act 2025)

Companies investing in AI are eligible for a double deduction on both capital and recurrent expenditure related to such investments. This incentive is limited to new or existing companies with an annual turnover not exceeding MUR 100 million (approximately USD 2.2 million). Effective from 2026.

Source: Finance Act 2025 (Act No. 18 of 2025); UNCTAD Investment Policy Monitor (measure 5284); KPMG Finance Act 2025 alert, 14 August 2025

10-year income tax holiday for startups — from day of operations

Under the Start-Up Act being introduced, startups will benefit from an income tax holiday of 10 years, applicable from the day of operations (not the date of incorporation). The ecosystem also includes: a dedicated Start-Up Act; a Hub at the Côte d'Or SEZ; a public-private Start-Up Council; an EDB Accelerator Scheme; a digital patent management system; and a dedicated start-up labour framework.

07

Golden Visa — USD 1 million investment, targets AI and fintech sectors

Mauritius introduced a Golden Visa programme requiring a minimum investment of USD 1 million within 12 months of arrival. PM Ramgoolam told lawmakers the programme was introduced following "multiple enquiries" from foreigners seeking to relocate with their families. The programme targets sectors including fintech, artificial intelligence, and biotechnology. Targeting 100 high-net-worth individuals per year. The visa provides multiple-entry rights for the holder, their spouse, and dependent children.

Sources: Bloomberg, 5 May 2026; Business Standard, 7 May 2026; BusinessDay NG, 9 May 2026; Fortune, 6 May 2026; Cabinet approval reported April 2026

Risk Assessment

Gaps and challenges — an honest evaluation

The following risks are drawn from analysis by PwC, KPMG, IQ-EQ, and MCCI on the Budget 2026–2027, and from the structural context of Mauritius's AI governance history (OECD.AI, UNDP). Each point is attributed.

📊

Execution track record

PwC Mauritius (Budget Brief 2026–27): "This Budget will ultimately be judged not by the breadth of its ambitions, but by the effectiveness of its execution." Budget 2025–2026 commitments (AI Unit, sector tools) were still being implemented at the time this budget was presented.

🏛️

MAIC governance gap persists

The Mauritius AI Council (MAIC), recommended in the 2018 AI Strategy, was not referenced in the Budget Speech 2026–2027. OECD.AI notes: "while the strategy was validated by a working group, it lacked a robust implementation plan, dedicated funding, or clearly defined accountability mechanisms." (OECD.AI Mauritius AI Strategy dashboard)

💰

Personal income tax rate vs talent attraction

IQ-EQ Budget Analysis (20 June 2026): "This appears somewhat inconsistent with revised personal income tax provisions, which raise the effective top rate to 35% on personal income exceeding Rs 12 million per annum — potentially discouraging high-income investors." This tension affects the Golden Visa's appeal to senior AI talent.

📋

SEZ incentive scheme detail pending

The Budget Speech states the SEZ incentive scheme "is being implemented" (BS §42) but does not specify the incentive structure. Investors cannot currently model the SEZ tax/duty benefit without further EDB guidance.

⚖️

FAIR Guidelines are non-binding

The FAIR Guidelines (April 2026) are currently non-binding — there are no penalties for non-compliance. The UNDP press release (10 April 2026) notes they are "expected to shape government policy, inform sector-specific regulations, influence procurement standards, and eventually underpin future legislation." Timeline for legislation not specified.

📏

Limited published performance framework

Beyond the 50,000 skills target and the USD 3bn export target for the SEZ, the Budget Speech does not publish a comprehensive AI performance dashboard or accountability timeline. KPMG (Budget Brief 2026–27): "The priority now is to translate fiscal discipline and structural reforms into stronger, more inclusive and sustainable growth."

Overall assessment

The Mauritius Budget 2026–2027 AI agenda represents a significant step up from the prior budget in ambition, specificity, and institutional commitment. The fiscal incentive stack is verified and competitive for a small open economy. The primary risks are execution-related (SEZ incentive details pending, MAIC gap, FAIR Guidelines non-binding) rather than fundamental — the strategic direction is sound. For investors, the correct posture is: the fiscal case is evidenced in law; the infrastructure enablers are being assembled; the governance environment is maturing but not yet fully stabilised. Independent legal and tax verification of specific incentive applicability is recommended before investment decisions.
